# Attribution

planetearth.live is a thin visualization layer. The **data** belongs to the originating scientific agencies and is used under their respective public-data terms. Please cite the agency, not this project, when you reuse numbers you saw here.

Suggested citation formats for each source follow. If you are an agency representative and want your citation changed, open an issue and we will update this file.

## Atmosphere

- **CO₂, CH₄, N₂O global monthly means** — Dlugokencky, E.J., K.W. Thoning, P.M. Lang, and K.A. Masarie. *NOAA Global Monitoring Laboratory Reference Network flask sampling program.* Available at <https://gml.noaa.gov/ccgg/trends/> (CO₂), <https://gml.noaa.gov/ccgg/trends_ch4/> (CH₄), <https://gml.noaa.gov/ccgg/trends_n2o/> (N₂O).
- **Global temperature anomaly** — GISTEMP Team, 2024. *GISS Surface Temperature Analysis (GISTEMP), version 4.* NASA Goddard Institute for Space Studies. <https://data.giss.nasa.gov/gistemp/>. *Accompanying paper:* Lenssen, N., G.A. Schmidt, J.E. Hansen, et al. (2019), *J. Geophys. Res. Atmos.*, 124. doi:10.1029/2018JD029522.
- **Kp index** — NOAA Space Weather Prediction Center (SWPC). *Planetary K-index.* <https://www.swpc.noaa.gov/products/planetary-k-index>.

## Air quality

- **PM2.5 (model)** — Copernicus Atmosphere Monitoring Service (CAMS), ECMWF. Accessed via Open-Meteo <https://open-meteo.com/> under the Open-Meteo CC-BY-4.0 redistribution license.
- **PM2.5 (stations)** — OpenAQ contributors. <https://openaq.org/>.
- **UV index** — Open-Meteo GFS. Same license as above.

## Cryosphere and oceans

- **Global sea-ice extent** — Fetterer, F., K. Knowles, W.N. Meier, M. Savoie, and A.K. Windnagel (2017, updated daily). *Sea Ice Index, Version 3.* National Snow and Ice Data Center (NSIDC). doi:10.7265/N5K072F8.
- **Global mean sea level** — Church, J.A., and N.J. White (2011). *Sea-level rise from the late 19th to the early 21st century.* Surveys in Geophysics, 32(4). Updated via NOAA/NESDIS satellite altimetry.
- **Tide gauge, The Battery, NYC** — NOAA Center for Operational Oceanographic Products and Services (CO-OPS), station 8518750. <https://tidesandcurrents.noaa.gov/stationhome.html?id=8518750>.

## Lithosphere

- **Earthquakes** — U.S. Geological Survey, Earthquake Hazards Program. *Real-time earthquake feeds.* <https://earthquake.usgs.gov/earthquakes/feed/>.

## Biosphere

- **Tree cover loss** — Hansen, M.C., P.V. Potapov, R. Moore, et al. (2013). *High-Resolution Global Maps of 21st-Century Forest Cover Change.* Science 342(6160), 850–853. Accessed via Global Forest Watch <https://data.globalforestwatch.org/>.
- **Biodiversity observations** — GBIF.org. *GBIF Occurrence Download.* <https://api.gbif.org/>.

## Energy

- **UK grid carbon intensity** — National Grid ESO + University of Oxford. *Carbon Intensity API.* <https://api.carbonintensity.org.uk/>.

## Reference / hardcoded datasets

- **World population** — UN DESA, *World Population Prospects 2024 Revision.* <https://population.un.org/wpp/>.
- **Cities (night-lights layer)** — Natural Earth, public-domain cultural vectors. <https://www.naturalearthdata.com/>.
- **Biodiversity hotspots** — Critical Ecosystem Partnership Fund, derived from IUCN hotspot definitions.
- **Measurement-station reference lists** — NOAA NDBC (buoys), NOAA CO-OPS (tide gauges), NOAA SURFRAD (surface radiation), NOAA GML (greenhouse gas observatories), Euro-Argo (Argo floats).
