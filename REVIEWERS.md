# Reviewers

This file lists the people who currently review pull requests against planetearth.live, in two tracks:

1. **Maintainers** — have commit access and merge rights (see `GOVERNANCE.md`).
2. **Scientific reviewers** — volunteer domain experts who are consulted on methodological questions. No write access; they review pull requests that touch methodology.

Each entry discloses a materially-relevant conflict of interest where one exists. Disclosure is not disqualifying — silence is. See `GOVERNANCE.md` § "Conflict of interest".

## Becoming a scientific reviewer

Scientific reviewers contribute their expertise at a low and flexible cadence — in practice, one or two PRs per quarter. We ask for:

- A short CV or a link to a public profile (ORCID, academic page, etc.) so readers of `REVIEWERS.md` know who signed off on a given methodological change.
- A written COI disclosure, even if there is no conflict. "None" is a valid and useful disclosure.
- Availability to respond to a tagged PR comment within 14 days, or to clearly indicate unavailability.

In exchange:

- Every PR the reviewer signs off on records the review in the merge commit, so the contribution is durable and citeable.
- Reviewers have final methodological say on disagreements in their domain (see `GOVERNANCE.md` § "Disagreement resolution").
- Reviewers are credited in the `CHANGELOG.md` entry for the release their review shipped in.

Email **hello@planetearth.live** to start the conversation.
